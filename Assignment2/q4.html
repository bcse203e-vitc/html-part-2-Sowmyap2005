DESTINATION

Athens

The Acropolis is one of the most significant and iconic landmarks in Greece and a symbol of ancient Western civilization. Situated on a rocky hill in the heart of Athens.

One of the largest temples of ancient Greece, dedicated to Zeus. Only a few columns remain, but they offer a glimpse into the grandeur of the past.

As the capital city, Athens is the cradle of Western civilization, boasting iconic landmarks such as the Acropolis and the Parthenon. The city seamlessly blends ancient history with modern urban life.

Monastiraki Square

Ermou Street is Athens' top shopping hub, blending global brands, local boutiques, and historic charm.

The Museum of the Ancient Agora in Athens displays artifacts from the city's political, social, and cultural hub, housed in the restored Stoa of Attalos.

TIPS

  • Best Time to Visit: The ideal time is during spring (April to June) or fall (September to October) for pleasant weather and fewer crowds.
  • Dress Comfortably: Wear comfortable shoes for exploring ruins and walking on uneven streets.
  • Stay Hydrated: Greece can get hot, especially in the summer, so always carry water with you.
  • Respect Local Customs: Greeks value politeness, so a friendly greeting in Greek ("Kalimera" for good morning) is appreciated.
  • Public Transport: Use ferries for island hopping and Athens' metro for easy city travel.
  • Currency: The local currency is the Euro (EUR), and credit cards are widely accepted.
  • Pack Light: If your visiting multiple islands, pack light since you will be moving around a lot.
  • Sunscreen: The sun can be intense, so use sunscreen and wear a hat or sunglasses.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Do not miss traditional Greek dishes like moussaka, souvlaki, and baklava.
  • Safety: Greece is generally safe, but always be cautious of your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
